FX & Rates Outlook

Recent market movers

Asset markets witnessed high volatility in May as investors navigated uncertainties around US debt ceiling, future course of monetary policy in the US and China’s economic recovery. In this edition, we have covered what were the major market movers in May and the way forward.

• US data: Recent data shows that inflation is moderating in the US, however, not at the pace required to achieve 2% inflation target set by the Federal Reserve. Going by inflation measure which the US central bank closely tracks, inflation stood at 4.4% in April vs. 4.2% earlier. In the labour market, the unemployment stood at 3.4% in Apr-23, closer to its historical low. This combination of high inflation and tight labour market conditions has raised expectations of more rate hikes and pushed back rate cut expectations in the US. We expect one more rate hike by the Fed in June or July, with increasing chances of a hawkish pause in June.

• Dollar view:The dollar index continues to remain bid both in risk-on and off scenarios and the rally seems to be beyond a pure “safe haven” play. In the near term, the dollar index is expected to remain supported, which could weigh on emerging market currencies as well as on the EUR, the JPY and the GBP. Over the medium term, a sustained moderation in the dollar is likely to take place only after the Fed commits to end its rate hike cycle. However, the moderation could be limited as elevated inflation and tight labour market, could decrease rate cut expectations.

• EUR Outlook:The EUR/USD pair fell by 2.7% in May-23. Apart from dollar strength, Eurozone specific factors have weighed on the pair. We expect the pair to remain under pressure in the near-term despite further rate hikes by the ECB. Near term range-1.06-1.08. Some reversal is likely towards the second half of the year towards 1.08-1.11 range as the dollar rally subsides and rate cuts expectations resurface.

• INR Outlook:After a strong rebound in Q1, China’s recovery has been weak, with which the USD/CNY pair fell by ~3% in May-23. With the increased correlation between the CNY and the INR, the INR has come under some pressure, against the dollar. However, healthy portfolio flows and two-way intervention by the RBI has kept the pair range-bound. We expect the pair to trade in the range of 82.50-83.0, in the run up to Fed’s June meeting. Over the medium term, we see the pair trading in the range of 80.50-81.50.

Notable Regulatory Changes

FinMin operationalises amnesty scheme for traders failing to meet export obligation:

• The finance ministry has operationalised the amnesty scheme for traders who defaulted in export obligation under the Advance and EPCG authorisation scheme. As per the amnesty scheme, which was announced in the Foreign Trade Policy on March 31, EXIM traders who failed to meet the export obligation under the Advance and EPCG (export promotion for capital goods) authorisation scheme can come clean by paying customs duty and interest, subject to a ceiling of 100 per cent.

• The Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC) asked principal commissioners/ commissioners to ensure that the exporters approaching for paying the duty, etc. are registered with the Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT).

Complete transition away from the London Interbank Offered Rate (LIBOR)

• The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has issued an advisory to banks and other RBI-regulated entities, emphasizing the need to take steps to ensure a complete transition away from the London Interbank Offered Rate (LIBOR) from July 01, 2023. The key messages in the advisory include:

• Banks / Financial Institutions (FIs) are advised to ensure that no new transaction undertaken by them or their customers rely on or are priced using the US$ LIBOR or the Mumbai Interbank Forward Outright Rate (MIFOR).

• The Financial Benchmarks India Pvt. Ltd. (FBIL) will cease to publish MIFOR after June 30, 2023.

• Banks/FIs are advised to take all necessary steps to ensure insertion of fallbacks at the earliest in all remaining legacy financial contracts that reference US$ LIBOR (including transactions that reference MIFOR).

• Banks/FIs are expected to have developed the systems and processes to manage the complete transition away from LIBOR.

The Reserve Bank will continue to monitor the efforts of banks/FIs for ensuring a smooth transition from LIBOR.

Remittances to International Financial Services Centres (IFSCs) under the Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)

• RBI has directed that “Resident Individuals may also open a Foreign Currency Account (FCA) in IFSCs, for making the above permissible investments under LRS.”

Thus, the condition of repatriating any funds lying idle in the account for a period up to 15 days from the date of its receipt is withdrawn with immediate effect, which shall now be governed by the provisions of the scheme as contained in the aforesaid Master Direction on LRS.
